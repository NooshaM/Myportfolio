Overview

The Problem

Not only the volume of online sales shows optimistic figures and projections, but also web and mobile-influenced offline sales are forecast to increase in the coming years. Despite the ever-growing rate of shopping and convenience it provides, some people continue to prefer to shop at physical retail stores. This is mostly because of the challenge of offering adequate physical evaluation of apparel items prior to purchasing them.

The Solution

A visual imagination can reduce the gap between the expectations and the actual performances of fashion products by offering sufficient complementary information of the feeling, fabric texture, quality and fit. This in in turn reduces the dissatisfaction that may lead to returning the purchased apparel. As a result, we developed a fashion apps that tries to visualize the outfit wearing and look.

CATEGORY

Web and Mobile design, Visual design.

Designing for a Responsive Web

Our biggest focus was to have a website that was accessible by mobile phones. To do this we needed to create a site that was quick and responsive. After identifying the problem we were able to move into the wireframe phase which allowed us to iterate quickly. Once we were happy with the result we brought some users in to test out our prototypes and gather their feedback.

Getting User Feedback

Putting high fidelity prototypes in front of our users allowed us to gather more accurate user feedback.

RESEARCH

Field Research/ Interview

We began the project by first trying to understand the customers and the problem space. We conducted research at online shopping websites in order to evaluate problem areas and opportunity areas. We also conducted a survey to uncover customer behaviors and pain points with their online shopping experiences.

User Persona

Based on our research, we created two types of User persona based on our interviews.

Empathy Map

Based on our research, we created two empathy maps for two types of customers.

Journey Map

Using what we know about the customers and the physical space in the stores, we created a shopping experience map. We identified the different touchpoints in the customer experience and outlined their pain points at different stages of the journey. This helped us uncover specific opportunity areas for design.

DESIGN

App User Flow

Once we determined the new customer journey for online shopping. I worked on the user flow of how the customer might interact with the app. Some of the things I had to consider were how the app would work with the visual imagination, how the user would use the “Visual body” feature, and how to enable customers to purchase items directly on the app successfully.

PROJET LEARNINGS

Understand the context in addition to the users

Learning about the customers in the online environment helped us understand the problem space better. The insights we uncovered in the research phase drove the design decisions of how the visualization would be integrated in the online shopping and how it would interact with the entire shopping experience.
